Our results & news

Meeting with Ms. Hiroko Niwa, President of Ryugasaki International Association, and Ms. Mariko Ukiyo, Representative of JME

16.12.2022

Julia Bernatska, founder of I Help Ukrainian Women, met yesterday with Ms. Hiroko Niwa, President of Ryugasaki International Association, and Ms. Mariko Ukiyo, Representative of JME. During the meeting, the issues of assistance to Ukrainians evacuated to Japan and those who remained in Ukraine were discussed.

One of the most important and effective methods of assistance was discussed at the meeting: targeted assistance to victims, as well as pre-qualification and the opportunity to obtain a new profession. "I Help Ukrainian Women" supports a policy aimed at providing new opportunities for Ukrainian evacuees. And, by the new year, we intend to prepare and launch a new IT specialty training course. Ukrainians' education will be free.

Employment in Japan

19.07.2022

In April 2022, our company BIJB launched the #Ihelpukrainianwomen initiative, the main goal of which was real help to Ukrainian women who were forced to flee the war.

One of the main directions is helping those girls who want to change their major and start working in IT.

Now we have an opportunity to help experienced IT specialists (both girls and boys) remaining in Ukraine with remote employment.

The work is sufficient.

Mandatory conditions of employment:

  1. Experience in web development from 3 years
  2. Free communication in English

I want to say that hiring workers, who do not live in Japan and do not even speak Japanese, is a new experience for Japan. There is such a practice here, but to a very limited extent.

Therefore, the fact that we were able to agree on such opportunities for Ukrainian developers is a big step in the beginning of the development of relations between Ukraine and Japan.

If you have the desire and the opportunity, then write to the mail contact@bijb.study

New bicycles for Ukrainians

15.07.2022

Japan is very supportive of Ukrainians both at the state and private levels. Thanks to the efforts of Sanshiro Fujimoto, Ukrainians in Tokyo and Chiba received 20 new bicycles.

In Japan, bicycles are the main form of transportation for  many residents. The attitude towards bicycles is serious.

They are registered and issued numbers like a car. The police can stop you at any time and check if you really are the owner of the bike.

This is such a necessary and important transport for us that some arrived today even before the opening of the storeüòÑ

Full course of therapy, free of charge

17.06.2022

It's hard to change your life now. It always comes with fear, stress, emotional distress.

Everyone has their own problems. Some are afraid of leaving the bombarded city, some send a CV, some cannot adapt in a new city, some miss home a lot, some feel guilty.

We want to help you cope with fear and anxiety. As part of #Ihelpukrainianwomen, we have partnered with a psychologist who has agreed to take 3 people for a full course of therapy, free of charge. Here is a  link to a free webinar on the subject.

100 tubes of cream will go to girls

10.06.2022

The sun is very strong inJ apan. The need to protect yourself from its rays determines the style of clothing, behavior and methods of protection. Protection must be multifactorial.

One of the steps is the mandatory use of sunscreen. Thanks to Namba Michiyo, our girls have the opportunity to use such creams. We were given 100 tubes of cream. Packing packages, we will distribute to girls in Tokyo, Chiba, Mitaka.

Free English language groups for girls

06.06.2022

Introducing an excellent English teacher and our volunteer at initiative #Ihelpukrainianwomen Sofia Belaya.

She will hold two free English language groups for those girls who want to work in the IT field.

Hello everybody. My name is Sophia. I am 31. And I am a mother of two wonderful sons. And I also help people to fall in love with the English language, as well as to learn it quickly and effectively. I offer in-depth study of the English language with enthusiasm and positivity. This is what distinguishes me as a tutor from courses and programs that help to learn a language.

I graduated from Dnipropetrovsk National University, Faculty of English Philology. I have been tutoring for about 7 years. In general, I will not promise "golden mountains". But if someone is interested and really needs effective help in learning English, contact them. I will be glad to help.

Meeting in the Odawara town

24.06.2022

Thanks to Valentyna Freedom for inviting us to a meeting in her town of Odawara. A warm welcome, sincere sympathy and interest from the Japanese people.

We talked about Ukraine for almost 3 hours. They talked about what they experienced themselves, about how those who remained in Ukraine live now.
